EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report analyzes customer metrics that directly address concerns about customer concentration risk and switching costs. Data demonstrates that despite customer concentration, retention metrics and switching costs create significant barriers to competitive displacement.

Key Findings:
- While top 10 customers represent 18% of revenue, Net Dollar Retention of 125% means expansion revenue exceeds new customer acquisition burden
- Customer churn of 8% (vs 12% industry average) demonstrates strong product-market fit
- Switching costs analysis shows average implementation cost of $1.2M per customer, creating significant lock-in
- Customer concentration composition changes YoY, indicating healthy diversification trends

SECTION 3: SWITCHING COSTS JUSTIFY HIGH NRR

3.1 Relationship Between Switching Costs and NRR

Theory: High switching costs should correlate with:
- High retention rates (customers stay)
- High expansion rates (customers expand to recoup switching costs)
- Long-term customer value

Evidence from XYZ Data:

Large Enterprise Customers (high switching costs):
- Gross Retention: 98%
- Net Dollar Retention: 140%
- Average revenue increase: $7M per customer annually
- Average tenure: 7.2 years
- Lifetime customer value: $50M+

Mid-Market Customers (medium switching costs):
- Gross Retention: 92%
- Net Dollar Retention: 115%
- Average revenue increase: $600K per customer annually
- Average tenure: 4.8 years
- Lifetime customer value: $3.5M

SMB Customers (low switching costs):
- Gross Retention: 85%
- Net Dollar Retention: 95%
- Average revenue increase: $3K per customer annually
- Average tenure: 2.1 years
- Lifetime customer value: $250K

Pattern: Higher switching costs → higher retention → higher expansion → higher lifetime value

3.2 Switching Costs Provide Competitive Moat

Competitive Advantage from Switching Costs:

1. Customer Retention:
   - 8% churn vs 12% industry average = 400bps advantage
   - On 12,000-customer base = 480 fewer customers lost per year
   - Revenue impact: $120M+ annually (at $250K ACE)

2. Expansion Leverage:
   - 125% NRR means expanding revenue faster than churn impact
   - Customers renew + expand despite competitive offerings
   - Higher switching costs = higher willingness to expand

3. Competitive Defensibility:
   - Competitors cannot easily displace entrenched customers
   - High switching costs create barriers to competitive displacement
   - We can compete on features/innovation, not just price

4. Predictable Revenue:
   - Low churn = predictable cash flows
   - High NRR = predictable expansion revenue
   - Allows for confident financial planning and investment
